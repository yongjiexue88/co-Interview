Deloitte doesn’t just care if you can code. They care if you can ship clean logic under pressure, sound like someone they can put in front of a client, and not flinch when a question goes sideways. If you're expecting LeetCode on autopilot, you'll get flattened.</p><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">Here’s precisely how it works.</p><h3 class="text-xl font-bold mb-6 text-white">Online Application + Recruiter Screening</h3><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">What happens after you submit your resume? You send your resume to Deloitte’s portal, or get a referral, or meet someone on campus who tells you to apply. Either way, it hits a recruiter’s inbox. They’re not grading your side projects like it's Hacker News; they’re asking if this person can explain what they built. Can they survive a client meeting without melting?</p><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">If you’re lucky, you’ll get a fast email reply. If you’re not, it’ll be radio silence until a random Thursday at 2:17 pm when they ask for a 30-minute call. That call is where they check for two things:</p><ul class="mb-6 space-y-2 pl-6"><li class="text-gray-300">Can you walk through what you’ve built without turning it into a TED talk?</li><li class="text-gray-300">Do your qualifications actually match what’s on your resume?</li></ul><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">Heads up: If you're hiding anything (degree, work authorization, etc.), it'll surface later and not in a good way.</p><h3 class="text-xl font-bold mb-6 text-white">Online Assessment</h3><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">What does the test really look like? It’s timed. It’s proctored. It’s not the moment to open 27 tabs and Google “binary search in Python.” You’ll get 4–5 sections in a row: quick logic questions, some algorithm stuff, and multiple-choice on <a href="https://www.uopeople.edu/blog/the-basics-of-computer-science-how-to-get-started/" target="_self" class="text-white underline decoration-gray-400 underline-offset-2 hover:decoration-gray-600">CS basics</a>. Expect 60–90 minutes of non-stop pressure.</p><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">No one cares if you can outsmart the grader with a weird trick. They want clean code that works. They're watching:</p><ul class="mb-6 space-y-2 pl-6"><li class="text-gray-300">How do you break problems down</li><li class="text-gray-300">How you handle feedback</li><li class="text-gray-300">Whether you panic when they change the question mid-way</li></ul><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">If you’re more senior, expect a mini systems design question. Mention tradeoffs if they’re real. And for the love of clean code, say what you’re thinking out loud.</p><h3 class="text-xl font-bold mb-6 text-white">Behavioral Interview</h3><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">Why does Deloitte ask these soft-skill questions? Use real stories. Don’t make them up. Be honest about your thinking process. They care more about your judgment than your title.</p><h3 class="text-xl font-bold mb-6 text-white">Offer + Negotiation</h3><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">What do they pay, and what should you say when the number drops?
