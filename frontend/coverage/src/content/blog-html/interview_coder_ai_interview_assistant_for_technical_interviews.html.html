Can You Explain The Difference Between C++ And JavaScript In Terms Of Memory Management?</h3><h4 class="text-lg font-bold mb-6 text-white">Rationale</h4><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">They want to know if you actually understand how things behave under the hood, not just “I used it in school once.”</p><h4 class="text-lg font-bold mb-6 text-white">How To Answer</h4><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">Contrast the strict, hands-on lifetime rules of C++ with JavaScript’s garbage collector. JavaScript runs on a garbage-collected VM, which saves you from whole categories of mistakes but can pause at bad times if you’re sloppy. If I were writing a renderer in C++, I’d lean on stack allocation and pools. In JS, I’d keep objects short-lived and watch for accidental retention with small benchmarks.”</p><h3 class="text-xl font-bold mb-6 text-white">2. Describe A Time You Optimized A Piece Of Code.
