Here’s what I’ve learned after sitting through enough of these:</p><h3 class="text-xl font-bold mb-6 text-white">What They’re Really Testing With Jira Questions</h3><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">They’re not just checking whether you know what an "epic" is. These questions are designed to sniff out real usage:</p><ul class="mb-6 space-y-2 pl-6"><li class="text-gray-300">Ever built a workflow from scratch?</li><li class="text-gray-300">Did you tweak notifications or set up automation rules?</li><li class="text-gray-300">Can you explain why you added a validator on a transition?</li></ul><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">They want examples. Not textbook answers. I once talked about how I cleaned up a bloated backlog by setting up a <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/jira-query-language-jql-savio-david" target="_self" class="text-white underline decoration-gray-400 underline-offset-2 hover:decoration-gray-600">Jira Query Language (JQL)</a> filter that flagged untouched tickets after 30 days. The interviewer didn’t say a word, just nodded and moved on. That’s when I knew I nailed it.</p><h3 class="text-xl font-bold mb-6 text-white">You’ll Get Thrown Into Scenarios: Here’s How to Handle Them</h3><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">“Let’s say your team is mid-sprint and suddenly bug reports double overnight. That’s Tuesday.</p><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">These scenario questions test your ability to use Jira under pressure effectively. Think:</p><ul class="mb-6 space-y-2 pl-6"><li class="text-gray-300">How you’d use filters to sort through the noise</li><li class="text-gray-300">What validators or conditions do you add to prevent chaos</li><li class="text-gray-300">Whether you involve a PM before changing a workflow (pro tip: always)</li></ul><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">There’s no perfect answer; they’re watching how you think.</p><h3 class="text-xl font-bold mb-6 text-white">Jira Questions Are Also About Your Agile Chops</h3><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">Most companies don’t run Jira in a vacuum. They run it with standups, retros, sprint planning, and all the other rituals. So, yeah, they’re going to ask:</p><ul class="mb-6 space-y-2 pl-6"><li class="text-gray-300">How do you use Jira to run sprint planning?</li><li class="text-gray-300">What do you check in a daily standup?</li><li class="text-gray-300">Can you explain what a velocity chart actually means?</li></ul><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">If you’ve ever been the person explaining burndown charts to a confused manager, you're golden here.</p><h3 class="text-xl font-bold mb-6 text-white">Collaboration: The Sneaky Skill Behind Jira Mastery</h3><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">Jira isn’t just tickets. It’s people trying not to drop the ball.</p><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">Interviewers want to hear how you’ve:</p><ul class="mb-6 space-y-2 pl-6"><li class="text-gray-300">Used @mentions and watchers to avoid miscommunication</li><li class="text-gray-300">Structured permissions so interns don’t accidentally delete a backlog</li><li class="text-gray-300">Linked Confluence docs so people didn’t have to beg for updates</li></ul><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">It’s not about being a Jira wizard.
