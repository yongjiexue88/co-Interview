The recruiter wants to know if you’re wasting their time or not. You’ll talk about past projects, what you owned, what you decided, and why. If you’re vague or try to make fluff sound like depth, you’re out. Sometimes</h3><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">If the role is more specialized, or the team wants to see how you handle messier, real-world problems, they’ll throw you a take-home. You’ll usually get 24–72 hours. They're not looking for cute tricks or clever hacks. Clean structure, clear thinking, basic tests, and a README that doesn’t sound like ChatGPT wrote it.</p><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">Hot tip: Over-explain your decisions. They’re checking how you think <em class="italic text-gray-300">with</em> people. Can you communicate? Can you disagree without being annoying? Can you work through an edge case without panicking?</p><p class="mb-6 text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">Netflix is allergic to groupthink, so they intentionally put you in situations where you need to hold a strong opinion and defend it well.</p><h3 class="text-xl font-bold mb-6 text-white">Timeline?
